Ethan Westerkamp

GIS MS Student - UW Madison. Conservation Biologist

About

I am a graduate of University of Wisconsin-Madison (M.S., GIS Development) and Michigan State University (B.S., Fisheries and Wildlife - Conservation Biology.) I grew up in North Carolina and Michigan and like getting out and exploring the natural world both near and far. My undergraduate focus on conservation and my positions in land management in federal, state, and non-profits exposed me to the importance of robust and accurate mapping technology led me to commit my professional career to GIS.

Experience

I have worked and served with various levels of government and non-profit oranizations, with the USDA, US Forest Service, CISMA, the Michigan Department of Natural Resources, Michigan State University, and Americorps. The tasks and training I undertook involved real world projects with tasks ranging from extensive spatial data collection in the field to its databse organization, analysis, and reporting. I learned various problem solving, organizational, teamwork, and bureaucratic navigation skills along with hardware and software operation and troubleshooting.
